Decision 1
Adopt COSCO as the internal Net BMPH and workload balance benchmark
Same terminal, same 4-crane mode, same equipment. COSCO achieves Net BMPH 119 vs MSC 93 — a 26-point gap driven entirely by better crane balance (CV 10% vs 45%). COSCO is proof that the infrastructure supports BMPH 100+. Use as the reference in monthly planning reviews.
COSCO Net BMPH 119 | MSC 93 | Gap: −26
Decision 2
Set KPI: maintain CV ≤ 40% for at least 80% of total working time during execution
Pre-planning crane moves is not feasible — stowage plans for import/export cargo dictate initial crane work. The focus must be on execution. When imbalance is detected during operations — crane idle time, crane breakdown, or crane speed differences — trigger resequencing or workload rearrangement to restore CV ≤ 40%.
Trigger: idle / breakdown / speed gap → Resequence
Decision 3
Coordinate with shipping lines to optimize vessel rotation and reduce effective CI where possible
Avg MSC CI is 4.7 vs COSCO 5.4, yet both operate at 4 cranes. When CI significantly exceeds the terminal's practical crane capacity (e.g. CI 6+ receiving 4 cranes), the gap cannot be closed by execution alone. Engage shipping lines to explore service rotation adjustments, berth window optimization, or vessel swaps that better align CI with available crane resources.
